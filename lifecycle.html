Eggs

Female Stag Beetles lay an average of 24 eggs, depositing them underground near rotting wood.

Assuming enough wood remains to support their offspring, females will often return to where they emerged to lay. The eggs are tiny â€“ measuring just a few mm long. They will develop for around 30 days before hatching, at which point a tiny, totally creamy-white larva will emerge. Within a few hours of hatching, the larva will develop the distinctive bright orange head that is characteristic of Stag Beetle larvae.
